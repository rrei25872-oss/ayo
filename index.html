🛡 Stay Safe in the Digital World

Online scams are becoming more common and convincing. This website was created to educate students about scams, how they work, and how to avoid becoming a victim.

Learn More

❓ What Is a Scam?

A scam is a dishonest scheme designed to trick people into giving away money, personal details, or access to accounts.

⚡ How Scams Work

Scammers pretend to be trusted organizations such as banks, online stores, or government agencies.

👤 Why People Fall for Scams

Scams pressure people into acting quickly and emotionally.

📝 Common Types of Scams

📧 Phishing Scams

Fake emails or messages asking for passwords or OTPs.

🛒 Online Shopping Scams

Fake sellers offer cheap items that never arrive.

🎁 Prize & Giveaway Scams

You "win" a prize but must pay a fee or give info.

🛡 How to Protect Yourself

Awareness and caution are your best defense online.

⚠ Warning Signs

  • Urgent messages
  • Requests for passwords or codes
  • Unknown links
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers

🧠 Smart Habits

Verify sources, protect personal info, and ask for help if unsure.

🧠 Student Interview Questions

Please answer the following questions to share your experience and thoughts about online scams.

1. How often do you use the internet for school or personal purposes?

2. Have you ever encountered an online scam? If yes, can you describe your experience?

3. What types of online scams are you familiar with (e.g., phishing, fake links, fake online stores)?

4. How confident are you in identifying whether a website or message is a scam?

5. What signs do you usually look for to determine if something online is a scam?

6. Do you think students your age are vulnerable to online scams? Why or why not?

7. What information or lessons about online scams do you think students need most?

8. How can a website help students learn more effectively about scam awareness?

9. What features would you like to see in a scam-awareness website (e.g., examples, tips, quizzes)?

10. Do you believe a web-based platform can help reduce the risk of students falling for online scams? Why?
